EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Geopolitical tensions, inflation and a decrease in demand from the Chi-nese market (Amed et al., 2025) are the current challenges the luxury watch sector must battle, struggling to fulfil customer’s needs by losing ground. Nevertheless, there is still a growth procrastinated for the upco-ming years revealing the opportunity for brands to enhance desirability through customer experience overcoming current global market challen-ges.


In case of the German heritage fine watch manufacture A. Lange & Söh-ne which shines through exceptional craftsmanship, German precision and exclusivity the introduction of a Digital Product Passport that is ent-irely based on Blockchain technology would enhance the customer ex-perience through various elements. The Digital Product Passport would embody all the watches’ information about its product lifecycle also wor-king as a certificate to prove the authenticity of the unique timepiece.

Furthermore, it would strengthen the relationship between customer and brand by fostering trust through transparency and immersing him into the brand’s story. With sustainability playing a crucial role in every product lifecycle today the DDP enables all stakeholders to make more sustaina-ble and ethical decisions benefiting from the Digital Product Passport’s big collection of data (King, Timms and Mountney, 2023).


However, there are still risks involved in the introduction of a Digital Pro-duct Passport for the German brand like a lack of trust in sharing data and information among all the stakeholders due to privacy and data se-curity reasons (Heeß et al., 2024) as well as the acceptance towards the new technology from the brand’s customers.

The brand is part of the French conglomerate Richemont which revealed in their annual report a loss of 13% compared to the year prior for their spe-cialist watchmaker’s division (Richemont, 2025). However, according to the report A. Lange & Söhne shows better resilience in comparison to their ot-her watch brands (Richemont, 2025), signalising the brand’s strong position in the watch market which still is prognosticated with a steady growth in re-venue over the next few years (Statista, 2025a). Moreover, “The State of Fa-shion Luxury” 2025 report revealed a growth of 2-4% in the Luxury Watches category until the year 2027 exposing the opportunity to increase desirability through customer experience in the watch sector which is currently battling the effects of geopolitical tensions, inflation and a decrease in demand from the Chinese market (Amed et al., 2025).


A. Lange & Söhne shows a stable position in the market staying true to their values not having any recent changes in strategy being confident in the brand’s spirit and distribution strategy. At Watches and Wonders 2025 in Geneva, which is one of the most relevant exhibitions for watchmakers on the planet, A. Lange & Söhne unveiled three new watches presenting highest craftsmanship and watch movement innovation, enforcing its status as one of the most exclusive and exceptional watch making brands of the world (Watches and Wonders 2025, 2025).


Watches and Wonders 2025 was also the stage for the strong competitors the brand has, especially brands from Switzerland, like Rolex, Patek Philip-pe and Audemars Piguet maintain a strong status and image profiting from their Swiss heritage and special craftsmanship. Further, competitors like Pa-nerai and Breitling strategically implemented key technologies for marketing purposes, where as A. Lange & Söhne is yet to implement technologies for their customer experience.

The Italian luxury watch brand Panerai introduced a Digital Passport for their watches giving every watch a unique digital identity through blockchain technology. Key benefits of this technology are the proof of identity, building trust in the product’s authenticity fighting the counterfeit market and the Genealogy giving the customers more insights about the watches’ history

(Panerei, no date). Other watch manufactures like the Swiss brands Breitling and Vacheron Constantin, which is also part of the Richmont group just like

A. Lange & Söhne, equally invested into blockchain-based Digital Product Passports in 2022 and 2019. Louis Ferla, the CEO of Vacheron Constan-tin argues that “Digital identities will transform the way one owns valuable assets, enabling owners to track the product lifecycle, share or prove ow-nership, and benefit from tailored services attached to each timepiece. On top of proof of ownership, our clients will benefit from Blockchain’s ability to store all information about a timepiece, including technical information, user guides, certifications, and product history” (Arianee, no date).

Furthermore, Tag Heuer and Jaeger LeCoultre put their focus on Augmen-ted and Virtual Reality creating immersive experiences with different focus areas. The Swiss competitor Rolex recently filed an application for a patent about integrating a NFT chip in their watches to store data and information working like a digital passport, providing traceability and proof of authenticity in a secure way (Corder, 2024). The introduction of a digital passport would also enable a reliable way to prove ownership of luxury watches which is becoming crucial as the numbers of stolen or missing watches more than tripled the last years (Wright, 2024).


With A. Lange & Söhne being one of the most exclusive watches manufactures worldwide, the introduction of a digital passport would provide a way of tackling counterfeiting and theft, as the consumer especially value the authenticity of their exclusive timepieces and further the possibility to foster customer experience through providing more information creating a secure testament connecting to the customers passion revolving around the watch.

STRATEGIC SUGGESTION

The strategic suggestion for the heritage brand A. Lange & Söhne is to in-troduce a Digital Product Passport that is based on a NFT (non-fungible Token) created using Blockchain technology, for every watch made following the aim to enhance the customer experience. This approach promises aut-henticity and transparency that is permanent and fixed through the power of a NFT being a unique differentiator saved in a blockchain (Wu et al., 2023).


The technology holds the potential to enhance the customer experience by fulfilling A. Lange & Söhne’s customer base values by building on the brand’s own values creating co-creation. Further, the EU introduced in 2024 a new regulation demanding from almost all products sold in the EU

to implement a Digital Product Passport to foster product transparency and foster sustainability (European Union, 2024). The full implementation of the DDP for all products hitting the EU market will come to effect in 2030, with the early introduction A. Lange & Söhne could attain a competitive edge and represent values like authenticity through full traceability and transparency.

Moreover, over 80% of luxury consumers desire a DDP for watches accor-ding to a survey conducted in 2024 (Mcdowell, 2024).


The customer base of the German watch manufacture values the crafts-manship and engineering of their unique timepieces along with the exclusive status the brand maintains. Understatement plays a major role for the High Net Wort Individuals that are the brand’s customers as they seek privacy and exclusive understated luxury experiences, that are not communicated to the public (WGSN, 2024). Especially luxury customers have the desire to participate in exclusive events creating loyalty (Brey, 2024) which is crucial during contemporary times of expanding competitors and a general luxu-

ry slowdown in consumer’s spending as luxury watch consumers prove to have a more value driven approach where people desire owning 10-20 gre-at timepieces instead of just accumulating a lot of watches (Swithinbank, 2025).

The implementation of digital product passport for A. Lange & Söhne would provide consumers with a range of information for the watch, centralised in the digital passport for transparency of the product’s whole lifecycle (Ospital et al., 2023) and essential information about repair, the certificates, guarantee and insurance (Arianee, no date). As A. Lange & Söhne is targeting passionate consumers who desire to understand what they buy (Goulard, 2022) the Digital Product Passport would deliver the right values like providing information and transparency about the watch and every detail is has embodied, as all the brand’s movements and complications require many hours of elaborate craftsmanship. This would connect the brand’s values of precision, engineering and highest craftsmanship with the customer’s values building brand trust through transparency which is resulting in brand loyalty (Yuana and Sutarso, 2021). Furthermore, a survey exposed that over 80% of luxury consumers would use the DDP as a certificate of authenticity (Mcdowell, 2024) which would be an essential value that the DDP adds for the customer experience as it embodies all the information secured on the Blockchain (Wan and Jiang, 2025) and is usable as a certificate of authenticity.


The transparency of comprehensive data about the product lifecycle enables stakeholders to make choices that are sustainable and follow regulations (Gieß and Möller, 2025). With a lot of A. Lange & Söhne watches being made of Gold and other special materials sustainability and traceability plays an important role, as for example gold mining causes environmental impacts that lead to deforestation (Ukpanah, 2024). Stakeholders can build a more sustainable supply chain and product lifecycle in combination with ethical principles through the use of the DDP (King, Timms and Mountney, 2023).


As mentioned before, luxury consumers want to participate in exclusive events (Brey, 2024), therefore owners of A. Lange & Söhne’s watch could benefit from exclusive invitations to brand events or having access to special offers because they own the NFT (non-fungible token) in form of the Digital Product Passport (ABC, 2023).

Plus, the A. Lange & Söhne App showcasing the Digital Product Passport on the customer’s phone would unlock a new customer touchpoint where the brand can enhance not only the customer experience but also directly the relationship through direct communication improving the service quality and personalization which plays a crucial role as 82% of high net-worth individuals seek “personalized service and the potential for consumers to create long lasting memories” (Steiger, 2024).


The Digital Product Passport in combination with an App for the phone enables

A. Lange & Söhne to directly communicate with the customer whether it is about a service appointment for the watch or special offers, improving the service quality (Bordignon and Ongaro, 2025). Fan et al. (2023) argue that service quality fosters the value perceived by the customer and improved relationship quality.


Overall, the introduction of the Digital Product Passport based on a NFT connects to several value elements based on the “Elements of Value Pyramid”, like “Informs”; “Avoids Hassles”; “Reduces Risk”; “Heirloom”; etc..

IMPLEMENTATION

Every new watch made by A. Lange & Söhne needs to be sold with a card showing a QR-code to gain access to the Digital Product Passport through a smartphone app. Because of that the watch brand needs to work toge-ther with an IT-company to set up the dedicated app.


Like mentioned before, Rolex is currently working on a solution to integrate a NFT chip directly into the watch instead of using a card (Corder, 2024).

This method provides a secure way to access the Digital Product Passport directly over the watch instead of scanning a card, nevertheless the integra-tion of a digital chip into an analogue watch would especially for A. Lange & Söhne create resistance from the customer’s as they value traditional crafts-manship of the watch movements without digital components which are also the brand’s core values.


It is crucial that the card that comes with the watch and the smartphone app resemble A. Lange & Söhne’s identity, due to the reason that identifica-tion through a consistent brand identity fosters brand loyalty and minimizes the impact negative information have on the customer (Elbedweihy et al., 2016).

The feasibility of the DPP can be reasoned by the Strategic Experiential Mo-dules (SEMs) framework developed by Schmitt (2009) who argues that the customer experience can be divided in five dimensions in forming the beha-viour and perception of the customer: Sense, Feel, Think, Act and Relate.

I case of the DPP the dimension “Think” plays the major role as the DDP essentially is a detailed database of the product where the consumer can immerse himself. Pine & Gilmore (1999) explain how decisive it is to have memorable experiences for the customer as it adds extra value and differen-tiation, which in the case of the DDP is added value through authentication, transparency, better service, etc.. Moreover, the success of the DDP for A. Lange & Söhne is supported by the Experiential Realm Model also designed by Pine & Gilmore, which argues that the customer’s experience varies in participation and on immersion through: Entertainment, Educational, Escap-sit and Esthethic (Pine & Gilmore, 1999). The educational and informative part in combination with an enjoyable user experience of the DDP connects to the Educational and Entertainment realm of the framework and supports the success and general feasibility of the implementation.

RISKS OF IMPLEMENTATION

Nevertheless, there are still risks worth considering implementing technolo-gy like th DDP in this case for the German heritage watch brand from Glas-hütte. The Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (UTAUT 2) is used to determine the consumer’s acceptance towards the DDP for A. Lange & Söhne, as the framework focuses on the consumer context.


Due to the rapid development of technology, various research models were introduced to explore the adoption of technology like the Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (UTAU) model that set the base for the later introduction of UTAUT 2 model which will be applied in this context.

UTAUT 2 adds hedonic motivation, price value and habit in comparison to the original model putting more emphasize on the consumer context (Tamil-mani et al., 2021).


Figure 13: UTAUT2 Model (Venkatesh, Thong and Xu, 2012)

As seen above Age, Gender and Experience are influential factors impacting Behavioral Intention with Experience also playing the role of directly influen-cing the Behavioral Intention in context of the final Use Behavior (Venkatesh, Thong and Xu, 2012). Following analysis (fig X.) based on the UTAUT 2 mo-dels shows great acceptance of the introduction of the DDP for the watch brand A. Lange & Söhne and holds the potential of further improvement and even fostering the acceptance through hedonic aspects like incorporating VR or Gamification in the future.


In addition, with data being one of the key aspects of the Digital Product Passport there is the risk of a lack of willingness for stakeholders to share data due to revelation and privacy concerns (Heeß et al., 2024). Not only the supplier has the mentioned concerns, a survey from 2023 shows high importance of data privacy worldwide among consumer with an increasing trend for older people putting more importance on the topic in comparison to younger generations (Statista, 2023a). Even though the DDP will be ba-sed on a blockchain there is still the potential for security risks, making it essential to protect the customer’s data with further steps minimizing con-cerns about data privacy for all stakeholders involved.

CONCLUSION

To conclude, the introduction of a Digital Product Passport for the German heritage watches brand A. Lange & Söhne would generate an authentic and innovative way to improve the customer experience through technology. Ne-vertheless, there are still risks involved that can be minimized by building on the DDP to further improve the customer experience and add value.


A. Lange & Söhne maintains an exclusive position in the market and strong reputation like seen in the VRIO analysis, having customers who have speci-fic values like heritage, precision, authenticity and a strong passion revolving around the art of building watches. The DDP offers the possibility to deliver values that are meaningful and count by enabling the customer to own an unique and exclusive testament about their watch which gives access to a small circle of people, based on the blockchain technology. Among the ad-ded values is also the sustainability facete which is important looking at the exclusive materials used to build an A. Lange & Söhne watch. Not only the brand experience is enhanced but also the attachment the customer has with their watch and the German brand through the emotional storytelling that is embedded in the app.


However, the UTAUT2 analysis in combination with the SWOT analysis ex-posed the potential the Digital Product Passport hold to even further enhan-ce the customer experience. Looking ahead, building on the Digital Product Passport further technologies can be introduced after the successful launch, like using Augmented Reality so the customer can further immerse himself in the story behind his watch and the brand’s heritage. The use of Augmen-ted Reality would enable a form of interactive digital storytelling (De Paolis

et al., 2022) by connecting the story world and the physical world we live in (Shilkrot, Montfort and Maes, 2014), to enhance customer engagement with the DDP and even raising the technological acceptance through hedonic features like mentioned before.

In addition, Gamification, which describes using “game elements in non-game contexts” (Suh, Wagner and Liu, 2018), have positive impact on the user’s experience and engagement (Raza et al., 2024) and therefore should also be incorporated on the DDP to further enhance the customer’s expe-rience. The key in the use of Gamification to enhance customer experience lays in satisfying psychological needs like self-expression and autonomy to enhance engagement (Suh, Wagner and Liu, 2018).


Essentially the Digital Product Passport would enhance the customer experience for the brand A. Lange & Söhne by adding meaningful value for the passionate customer holding high appreciation for the brand’s herita-ge, exclusivity and exceptional craftsmanship, differing from the competitor through complete traceability, emotional storytelling and by creating an ex-clusive testament enabling the customer to fully immerse himself in the ex-clusive world of A. Lange & Söhne.
